Muhammad Ali "the founder"

Nicknamed Al-Aziz or Aziz Egypt, he is the founder of the Alawi dynasty and ruler of Egypt from 1805 to 1848. He is commonly described as the "founder of modern Egypt", a saying that he himself was the first to promote and that continued after him in an orderly and striking manner. He was able to ascend the throne of Egypt in 1805 after the country's notables pledged allegiance to him to be a ruler over it, after the people revolted against his predecessor Khurshid Pasha, and his intelligence and exploitation of the circumstances surrounding him enabled him to continue to rule Egypt for that period, breaking the Ottoman custom that did not leave a ruler on Egypt for more than two years.

facade of the mosque

One of the famous ancient mosques in Cairo. It was established by Muhammad Ali Pasha between the period from 1830 AD to 1848 AD in the Ottoman style, similar to the Sultan Ahmed Mosque in Istanbul. It is sometimes called alabaster or alabaster mosque because of the frequent use of this type of marble in cladding its walls. The successors of Muhammad Ali Pasha took care of the mosque, so they completed the building and added some simple additions to it. They also made it a headquarters for the celebration of annual religious occasions. They were, in order, Abbas Hilmi Pasha I, Muhammad Said Pasha, Ismail Pasha, and Tawfiq Pasha. However.

Date of creation of the mosque

The oldest mention of the Mosque of Muhammad Ali Pasha dates back to the year 1236 AH-1820 AD, where the engineer Pascal Coast mentioned that Muhammad Ali asked him for designs for two mosques, one building the Citadel of Salah al-Din, and the second in the city of Alexandria, while the orders and correspondences of the mosque mention that the foundation stone for the building was laid on Thursday 19 Jumada al-Ula for the year 1244 AH / 1828 AD, and another architect named Youssef Bushnaq prepared a design for the mosque inspired by the Sultan Ahmed Mosque in Astana in 1241 AH-1828 AD,It took about 3 years, official implementation began in 1246 AH-1830 AD.

Architectural design of the mosque

The mosque as a whole consists of a rectangular shape that is divided into two parts, the eastern part; It is the prayer house or the mosque, and it is the place prepared for prayer. It is a square shape, the length of its side is 41 m, and in the middle is a high central dome, which is one of the largest domes in Islamic architecture in Egypt. Its diameter is 21 m, and its height is 52 m. It is carried on 4 large arches whose pillars are based on 4 square pillars. Huge, 11m high.

Mosque Square

A large courtyard measuring 53 m x 54 m, accessed from the door in the middle of the sea wall, and beneath it is a large cistern. It is surrounded by four arcades with arches carried on marble columns, bearing small domes carved from the inside and covered from the outside with lead panels like the large dome and with copper crescents . In the circles of the iwans, 46 windows overlook the outside of the mosque from the sea, western and tribal sides. As for the eastern side, it overlooks the mosque.

mosque dome from the inside

It has a marble crescent, engraved with prominent decorations of bunches of grapes, and it has a colorful engraved style written in Persian calligraphy with the Sinklakh pen. In the middle of the western portico of the court is a tower made of perforated copper and decorated with inscriptions and colored glass.
